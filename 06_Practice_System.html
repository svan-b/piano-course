Volume 6: The Practice System

The anatomy of your 30 minute morning session, and the complete 48 week plan that ties all six volumes together.

Why 30 daily minutes is enough

Motor skills consolidate between sessions, largely during sleep, so seven short exposures beat one long one by a wide margin, this is the spacing effect, one of the most replicated findings in learning science. Morning practice adds two more advantages: it happens before the day can steal it, and mistakes corrected today return tomorrow morning already half fixed. The compounding is remarkable: 30 minutes daily is 182 hours in a year, delivered in the way the brain likes best.

But the minutes only count if they are deliberate: each block below has a specific goal, immediate self feedback (your ears, the metronome, the checklist), and difficulty pitched at the edge of your ability. Drifting through pieces you already know feels like practice and does almost nothing. The plan exists so that every morning you sit down already knowing exactly what today's edge is.

The 30 minute session

2 min
Arrive. Posture reset, shoulders drop, 6 to 8 arm weight drops per hand. This is not a formality, it loads the physical habits that make the next 28 minutes safe and productive.
8 min
Technique. This week's scale, arpeggio, or exercise from the plan below, using the Volume 3 exercise bank and the speed ladder. Log your top clean tempo.
6 min
Reading and harmony. 3 minutes flashcards or sight reading (one new line, never repeated), 3 minutes chord work from Volume 4: building chords, inversion drills, or this week's progression.
12 min
Repertoire. The current piece, using the Volume 5 system: one section, hardest bars first, hands separate before together, ladder to tempo. Know your target before you start the timer.
2 min
Close. Play something you love, any standard. Then one line in the practice log, including tomorrow's first target.
Low energy days: the 15 minute kernel is 2 warmup, 5 technique, 8 repertoire. It keeps the chain alive, which matters more than any single session's content. Weekends, optional: add a second sitting for production work, record your loops and voicings into the DAW while they are fresh under your fingers.

How to use the plan

The week numbers are a sequence, not a deadline. Repeat any week freely; most people repeat several, and a "week" that takes ten days is normal and fine. The only rule is never skip forward past something unfinished, the plan compounds.

Milestones every 4 weeks (shaded rows) are honest self tests. Pass them and move on with confidence. Miss one, spend another week, no drama.

Read the referenced volume sections before their week begins, reading is free, it does not spend practice minutes. Evenings and coffee breaks are perfect for this.

The practice log

One line per day, kept wherever is frictionless, Notes app, a paper pad on the piano, anything. The format:

date | technique: what + top clean tempo | reading | piece + section | win | tomorrow's first target

Jul 14 | G maj HT ♩=76 | drill 4 clean | Grace bars 5-8 HT | pedal finally smooth | Grace bar 7 LH alone

Two log habits with outsized returns: always write tomorrow's first target (you start tomorrow already moving), and record your top clean tempo for whatever is on the ladder (progress you can see kills more motivation dips than any pep talk).

Troubleshooting

Plateau on a piece: you are probably practicing the parts you can play. Spotlight rule: two hardest bars, ten slow perfect reps, plus the join bars either side. If a passage refuses for a week, drop the tempo by a third, or return to hands separate for three days. It always yields.

Tension creeping back: re-read Volume 1 §2-3 and Volume 3 §1 with fresh eyes, they read differently after months of playing. Film your hands. Drop volume of everything to mp for two days, tension hides in loudness.

Boredom: boredom means insufficient challenge or insufficient music. Add one piece you simply love (any genre, any source), and check you are using the ladder honestly rather than cruising.

Missed days: one missed day costs nothing, restart. After a week away, spend two days at 80 percent difficulty before resuming the plan. Never punish yourself with extra long sessions, they borrow from tomorrow.

A week that will not pass: repeat it. Weeks are difficulty grades, not calendar promises. The plan still completes inside 14 months at a leisurely repeat rate, and that outcome remains excellent.

After week 48

You will be roughly at grade 3 to 4 level in the RCM/ABRSM sense, with unusually strong harmony skills for that level thanks to the production track. Year two options, mix freely: work toward a formal grade exam if external structure appeals, take the Burgmüller Op. 100 set deeper as your technique spine, start Bach's easier two part inventions for a counterpoint apprenticeship, expand the Clementi sonatinas, and let the improvisation and production thread grow, you now have the hands and the harmony to write real music. A monthly teacher checkup in year two is worth more than weekly lessons in year one, you will arrive with precise questions.
